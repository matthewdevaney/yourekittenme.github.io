Disqus Comments With Pelican

March 15, 2019 , Category: Web Development , Tags: disqus , pelican

Building a custom comment system on a static website is just not possible: there's no database back-end to host one. You can implement comments on your website but the trick is to use a service that will host it for you. Disqus is a popular solution to this problem. Small websites can use the service for free and installation/maintenance is quite painless. Another interesting feature is Disqus users can comment on your website and others using the same account. The catch is you have to pay a fee if you don't want ads served to your visitors and beyond a certain-level of traffic upgrading to pro is mandatory. If you can accept the trade-offs I believe Disqus is an excellent way to get the conversation started on your website.

In this mini-tutorial I will show you how to install Disqus on a static website created with Pelican.

Prequisites

You will need to sign up for the free version of Disqus

Goals

  • Allow comments using Disqus on your article pages

Installing Disqus

Once you have signed up for a Disqus account the go ahead and create a new site. Make a note of the new unique Disqus URL that will be generated. We will be adding it our Pelican settings file in a few moments.

Select the basic plan from the list of plans. Its free and you can always upgrade later in needed.

When prompted to select a platform choose I don't see my platform listed.

Fill-in our website URL on the configuration screen and complete the form. If you don't want go through all the settings now that's OK. You can come back and finish setup later.

Disqus comments will only appear on our published Pelican website. Open publishconf.py and uncomment the DISQUS_SITENAME variable. Replace the Disqus site name below with your own.

DISQUS_SITENAME = 'youre-kitten-mes-blog'

Now generate the website using publishconf.py

pelican content -s publishconf.py
pelican --listen

Open up the website in your browser and browse to an article. Disqus comments will load at the bottom of the page.
